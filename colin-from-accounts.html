Subverting the
Modern Rom-Com

By Ronan Sullivan Review

Patrick Brammall and Harriet Dyer's hit Australian comedy *Colin From Accounts* manages to breathe refreshing, grounded life back into a genre often choked by polished clichés. The narrative sparks when two flawed, mid-crisis strangers are brought together by an injured dog.

What sets the show apart is its unflinching commitment to awkward authenticity. The dialogue doesn't feel like scripted punchlines; it feels like the messy, defensive, and genuinely funny ways real people try to connect when they are completely out of their depth.

By bypassing the heavily sanitized tropes of modern streaming sit-coms, *Colin From Accounts* finds its heart in genuine vulnerability. It proves that character-driven humor and sharp writing are still the ultimate tools for creating a romantic comedy that audiences will actually care about.
